Solutions for Water Waste

Awareness Campaigns

Awarness Campaigns are one of the most effective way to reduce water wastage. LPU can organize campaigns to educate students and staff about the importance of water conservation and the impact of their actions on the environment. The university can also provide tips on how to reduce water usage, such as turning off the tap while brushing teeth, fixing leaky taps, and using low-flow showerheads. The awareness campaigns can be organized through various mediums, including social media, posters, banners, and workshops.

Install Low-Flow Fixtures

A low-flow fixture is a water saving plumbing fixture designed to achieve water savings by having a lower flow rate of water or a smaller quantity per flush. Some of these low-flow fixtures are faucets, showerheads, and toilets.Low-flow showerheads and faucets can reduce water usage by up to 50%. These fixtures can be installed easily and inexpensively, and they can save hundreds of gallons of water each year.

Fix Leaks Promptly

Water is a basic need of our life and is amply provided by the Governmental agencies. However, we as consumers do not prioritize on water wastage and leakages issue due to the subsidized water cost. The leakages from taps, valves and appurtenant are often noticed but the invisible leakages under the sink often goes un-noticed and drips occurring are not taken seriously and addressed till the leakage get larger and leak get worse.A leaky faucet can waste up to 3,000 gallons of water per year. Fix leaks as soon as you notice them to save water and prevent water damage to your home.

Use a Rain Barrel

Stormwater pollution happens when chemicals and sediments get picked up by rushing rainwater and flow into a storm sewer system or a local body of water. Rain barrels reduce this pollution by capturing and containing rainwater, decreasing the total amount of runoff water. Collecting rainwater in a barrel can be an easy and cost-effective way to save water. The water can be used for watering plants, washing cars, and other non-potable uses.

Greywater Recycling

LPU can recycle greywater for non-potable uses such as flushing toilets, landscaping, and irrigation. Greywater is wastewater generated from sinks, showers, and washing machines. The university can install a greywater treatment plant to treat and recycle greywater for non-potable uses. This would reduce the demand for freshwater and minimize wastewater discharge. Greywater recycling can significantly reduce the water footprint of the university. The university can collaborate with experts and organizations that specialize in greywater recycling to design and install a cost-effective greywater treatment plant. Drought-Tolerant Plants

LPU can upgrade its infrastructure to reduce water wastage. For example, the university can install sensor-based taps and toilets that automatically turn off when not in use. This would eliminate the need for students and staff to manually turn off the taps, reducing water wastage. The university can also install low-flow showerheads, which can save up to 50% of water used during showers.The upgrade of infrastructure can be a significant investment, but it will pay off in the long run through reduced water bills and increased sustainability. The university can apply for funding from government agencies and international organizations that promote sustainable development. The university can also collaborate with environmental experts and architects to design sustainable buildings that promote water conservation.
